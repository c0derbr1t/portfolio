What is this?

This is a daily project where we were given instructions on GitHub to follow. The purpose of this assignment is "Utilizing DOM manipulation as well as our understanding of events and event listeners to create elements with user interaction."

What kind of build was this?

Daily projects can be completed alone or by pair programming. We were encouraged to reach out to our Team Lead or other students for help as needed. We could not receive help with them. They reinforce the day's lesson, provide practice for the material, and expand your knowledge through self learning.

What did I do?

We were told to have fun with this one, and I definitely did! There are a lot of little things that happen on the page based on clicks, hovers, and other events. There are also several buttons on the bottom that change the page colors and pictures.

I set up my preprocessor. We were given suggestions on different event listeners we could possibly use, and had to add at least 10 unique event listeners to the page. I researched different ones, and made an obnoxiously fun site that has several different available themes.

What would I like to implement in the future?

Honestly, I like this particular project the way it is, for the most part. It is not refined at all, but it show's the level of skill I had at that particular point in time.I would like to figure out how to get the bus to scroll backwards and forwards as you scroll though!
